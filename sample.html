I like the idea of providing regional government. This usually gets dismissed as being "another layer of government that has to be paid for", and debate gets no further than that. Yet if we want good government which people can relate to, it needs to be paid for, be consistent across the country, and be near enough to the people being governed. At present we have unelected bodies which are supposed to look after regional interests in Westminster and Brussels. In the North West where I live, that's the North West Development Agency, and there's another, newer body called 4NW which is referred to as the "Regional Leader Board". Although some elected individuals (for example from local councils) get appointed to sit on these bodies, the electorate has no say over who they are and how they are appointed. There are also a load of unelected members who come from business. These agencies cost money to run. I think they should be abolished and the money saved put towards providing elected regional assemblies.

Doing so would provide the opportunity for many more citizens to get involved in the democratic process. It would solve the problem at Westminster of Scots MPs voting in Parliament on purely English issues. These issues would instead be decided in accordance with a region's priorities - in the way Wales, Scotland and Northern Ireland deal with things right now. This beats the idea of having an English parliament - the needs and interests of the North of England are very different from those of Devon and Cornwall.

Of course, the Tories say they will do away with regional agencies altogether - to save money. This would leave the regions with no voice in Westminster, but perhaps most importantly, in Europe. Given that, for example, Merseyside had massive investments from Europe which our own central government would never have provided, it's essential that we continue to be heard in both.

My ideal model would be a Westminster parliament for the whole UK dealing with foreign affairs, defence, intelligence and the like. Transport, education, health and such like would be devolved to the regions. The savings in a reduced Westminster and from the regional agencies would off set some of the cost.

Local government should be more local too. Things haven't really been right since many councils were amalgamated in the mid seventies. There are towns with thousands of people with no truly local council to look after things that should be decided locally. Cleansing and rubbish collection, parks and gardens, street signage etc., are now often dealt with by some "unitary" body quite remote from the people being governed.
